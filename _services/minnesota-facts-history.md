        <!-- History Section -->
        <h2 class="govuk-heading-l">History</h2>

        <details class="govuk-details" data-module="govuk-details">
          <summary class="govuk-details__summary">
            <span class="govuk-details__summary-text">
              <strong>Indigenous History</strong> (Pre-1800s)
            </span>
          </summary>
          <div class="govuk-details__text">
            <p class="govuk-body">
              Minnesota has been home to Indigenous peoples for thousands of years. The Dakota (Sioux) and Ojibwe (Chippewa) nations were the primary inhabitants when European explorers arrived.
            </p>
            <div class="govuk-inset-text">
              Today, Minnesota is home to 11 federally recognized tribal nations: four Dakota communities and seven Ojibwe reservations.
            </div>
            <ul class="govuk-list govuk-list--bullet">
              <li>Dakota peoples inhabited the southern and western regions</li>
              <li>Ojibwe peoples migrated to the northern forests and lake regions</li>
              <li>Both nations had complex societies, trade networks, and spiritual traditions</li>
              <li>European contact began in the 1600s with French explorers and fur traders</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </details>

        <details class="govuk-details" data-module="govuk-details">
          <summary class="govuk-details__summary">
            <span class="govuk-details__summary-text">
              <strong>European Exploration and Settlement</strong> (1600s-1800s)
            </span>
          </summary>
          <div class="govuk-details__text">
            <p class="govuk-body">
              European exploration of Minnesota began with French fur traders in the 17th century. The region changed hands between French, British, and eventually American control.
            </p>
            <h3 class="govuk-heading-s">Key Events:</h3>
            <ul class="govuk-list govuk-list--bullet">
              <li><strong>1679-1680:</strong> French explorer Daniel Greysolon, Sieur du Lhut, visits the region</li>
              <li><strong>1763:</strong> Region east of Mississippi passes to British control</li>
              <li><strong>1783:</strong> Eastern Minnesota becomes part of the United States</li>
              <li><strong>1803:</strong> Western Minnesota acquired through Louisiana Purchase</li>
              <li><strong>1819:</strong> Fort Snelling established at the confluence of Mississippi and Minnesota rivers</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </details>

        <details class="govuk-details" data-module="govuk-details">
          <summary class="govuk-details__summary">
            <span class="govuk-details__summary-text">
              <strong>Minnesota Territory and Statehood</strong> (1849-1858)
            </span>
          </summary>
          <div class="govuk-details__text">
            <p class="govuk-body">

        <details class="govuk-details" data-module="govuk-details">
          <summary class="govuk-details__summary">
            <span class="govuk-details__summary-text">
              <strong>Civil War and Dakota War</strong> (1861-1865)
            </span>
          </summary>
          <div class="govuk-details__text">
            <div class="govuk-warning-text">
              <span class="govuk-warning-text__icon" aria-hidden="true">!</span>
              <strong class="govuk-warning-text__text">
                <span class="govuk-visually-hidden">Important historical context</span>
                This period includes tragic events and injustices against Dakota peoples. We present this history to acknowledge these wrongs and honor those who suffered.
              </strong>
            </div>
            <p class="govuk-body">
              The Civil War era was tumultuous in Minnesota, including the U.S.-Dakota War of 1862, one of the darkest chapters in state history.
            </p>
            <ul class="govuk-list govuk-list--bullet">
              <li>Minnesota was the first state to offer troops to the Union (April 1861)</li>
              <li>Over 24,000 Minnesotans served in the Civil War</li>
              <li>The U.S.-Dakota War (1862) resulted from broken treaties and starvation</li>
              <li>38 Dakota men were executed in Mankato, the largest mass execution in U.S. history</li>
              <li>Most Dakota people were expelled from Minnesota after the war</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </details>

        <details class="govuk-details" data-module="govuk-details">
          <summary class="govuk-details__summary">
            <span class="govuk-details__summary-text">
              <strong>Immigration and Growth</strong> (1860s-1920s)
            </span>
          </summary>
          <div class="govuk-details__text">
            <p class="govuk-body">
              Minnesota experienced massive immigration and economic growth in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
            </p>
            <h3 class="govuk-heading-s">Major Immigrant Groups:</h3>
            <ul class="govuk-list govuk-list--bullet">
              <li><strong>Scandinavian:</strong> Norwegians, Swedes, Danes, Finns (largest group)</li>
              <li><strong>German:</strong> Second largest immigrant population</li>
              <li><strong>Irish:</strong> Significant presence in urban areas</li>
              <li><strong>Eastern European:</strong> Poles, Czechs, Slovaks</li>
              <li><strong>Southern European:</strong> Italians, Greeks</li>
            </ul>
            <div class="govuk-inset-text">
              By 1910, over 70% of Minnesota's population was either foreign-born or had foreign-born parents, giving the state its distinctive cultural character that persists today.
            </div>
          </div>
        </details>

        <details class="govuk-details" data-module="govuk-details">
          <summary class="govuk-details__summary">
            <span class="govuk-details__summary-text">
              <strong>Modern Era</strong> (1920s-Present)
            </span>
          </summary>
          <div class="govuk-details__text">
            <p class="govuk-body">
              Minnesota evolved from an agricultural and mining economy to a diverse, technology-driven state.
            </p>
            <h3 class="govuk-heading-s">20th Century Highlights:</h3>
            <ul class="govuk-list govuk-list--bullet">
              <li><strong>1920s-1930s:</strong> Rise of cooperative movement and progressive politics</li>
              <li><strong>1944:</strong> Democratic-Farmer-Labor Party (DFL) formed</li>
              <li><strong>1950s-1960s:</strong> Growth of medical technology industry</li>
              <li><strong>1960s-1970s:</strong> Civil rights movements and social reforms</li>
              <li><strong>1980s-1990s:</strong> Technology boom, including founding of major corporations</li>
              <li><strong>2000s-Present:</strong> Continued diversification and immigration from Asia, Africa, and Latin America</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </details>

            </span>
          </summary>
          <div class="govuk-details__text">
            <p class="govuk-body">
              Minnesota has a continental climate with extreme temperature variations between seasons.
            </p>
            <ul class="govuk-list govuk-list--bullet">
              <li><strong>Winter:</strong> Average lows of -10°F to 10°F (December-February)</li>
              <li><strong>Spring:</strong> Rapid warming, unpredictable weather (March-May)</li>
              <li><strong>Summer:</strong> Warm and humid, 70-85°F (June-August)</li>
              <li><strong>Fall:</strong> Cool and crisp, beautiful foliage (September-November)</li>
            </ul>
            <div class="govuk-warning-text">
